Eternity: A Movie Review

February 22, 2026 · 5 min read

So I watched Eternity on Apple TV the other night, and I just need to talk about it.

I wasn't expecting much, honestly. I'd scrolled past it a few times, thought the poster looked pretty enough, and figured it was going to be one of those slow-burn romances that takes itself too seriously. But I was in my cozy girl era that evening — blanket, tea, no plans — so I pressed play.

And oh, I'm so glad I did.

The Vibe

This movie is a romance, yes, but it's got this lightness to it — this quiet, playful comedy that sneaks up on you when you least expect it. It's not trying to make you laugh out loud (though it does, more than once). It's more like that warm, butterflies-in-your-stomach kind of humor — the kind that comes from two people being awkward and real and slightly ridiculous with each other.

You know that feeling when you're watching a couple on screen and you just get it? Like, you understand why they're drawn to each other, and you're rooting for them from the very first glance? That's what this movie does. It pulls you in gently, and by the time you realize how invested you are, it's too late. You're already in your feelings.

The kind of movie that makes you want to be in love, and also makes you perfectly fine watching love from under a blanket with a cup of tea.

The Chemistry

The leads have this effortless thing between them. It's not overdone. There are no dramatic airport chases or rain-soaked confessions (well — not the cliché kind, anyway). Instead, it's all in the small moments — the looks across a room, the pauses that say more than dialogue ever could, the way one of them tries to play it cool and completely fails.

That's where the comedy lives, by the way — in the failing to play it cool. In the "I'm totally fine" energy when they are very clearly not fine. I related to that more than I'd like to admit.

The Romantic Bits

Without giving too much away — this film understands romance. Not the Hollywood version where everything is grand gestures and perfect timing. The real kind. The kind where someone remembers something small you said weeks ago. The kind where silence between two people isn't awkward — it's full.

There's a scene in this movie that made me put my tea down, pull my blanket up to my chin, and just breathe. If you know, you know. And if you don't — go watch it and come back and tell me which scene it was for you.

The Comedy

I keep calling it "a bit of comedy" because it's not a comedy in the traditional sense. It's more like — life is funny, people are funny, falling for someone is inherently a little ridiculous, and this movie honors that. There were moments where I laughed not because of a punchline but because the situation was just so human.

The supporting characters add so much too. There's always that one friend character who says what the audience is thinking, and this movie nails it without making it feel forced.

Sometimes you don't need a movie to change your life. You just need it to make your night a little softer.

Final Thoughts

Eternity isn't trying to reinvent the romance genre. It's not groundbreaking or edgy or trying to subvert your expectations at every turn. What it is — is lovely. It's warm. It's the kind of movie that makes you want to text your person, or hug your pillow, or journal about what love means to you at this stage of your life.

I watched it alone on my couch on a random weeknight, and it was genuinely one of the best movie nights I've had in a while. Sometimes that's all you need — a good story, good chemistry, and a little bit of laughter tucked between the tender moments.

My rating: 8/10 — Would absolutely watch again on a cozy night in.

If you've seen it, come talk to me about it. And if you haven't — what are you waiting for? Go press play.
