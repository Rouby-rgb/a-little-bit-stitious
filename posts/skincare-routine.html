My Holy Grail Skincare Routine for Glowing Skin

February 8, 2026 · 6 min read

If there's one thing I'm truly passionate about (besides The Office, obviously), it's skincare. I've spent years testing products, reading ingredient labels, and slowly building a routine that actually works for my skin. Today, I'm sharing the full thing — every single product and step.

Why Skincare Matters to Me

Growing up in Accra, I dealt with the classic combination of heat, humidity, and skin that couldn't quite decide if it was oily or dry. For the longest time, I thought good skin was just about genetics. Spoiler: it's not. It's about consistency, finding what works for your skin, and being patient with the process.

I remember the first time someone complimented my skin at church. I had just started double cleansing a month prior, and honestly, I almost cried. That moment solidified it for me — taking care of my skin wasn't vanity, it was self-care.

My Morning Routine

Mornings are all about hydration and protection. I keep it simple because who has time for a 12-step routine before 7 AM?

Step 1: Gentle Cleanser

I start with a gentle, hydrating cleanser. Nothing foamy or stripping — just something that removes the overnight buildup without making my skin feel tight. I massage it in for about 60 seconds (yes, I time it), then rinse with lukewarm water. Hot water is the enemy, trust me.

Step 2: Vitamin C Serum

This is my non-negotiable. Vitamin C brightens, evens out skin tone, and gives that gorgeous "lit from within" glow. I pat 3-4 drops onto slightly damp skin and let it sink in for a minute. The key is to use it before your moisturizer so it can really penetrate.

Step 3: Lightweight Moisturizer

During the day, I go for something lightweight and gel-like. It keeps my skin hydrated without feeling heavy under sunscreen and makeup. I've found that heavier creams tend to make me look oily by midday, especially in Ghana's heat.

Step 4: Sunscreen (The Most Important Step!)

I cannot stress this enough — sunscreen is the most important step in any skincare routine. Yes, even for dark skin. Yes, even on cloudy days. I use a broad-spectrum SPF 50 that doesn't leave a white cast. I reapply every 2-3 hours if I'm outdoors.

My Evening Routine

Night time is when the real magic happens. This is where I focus on repair, nourishment, and treating any specific concerns.

Step 1: Oil Cleanser (Double Cleanse Part 1)

I start with an oil-based cleanser to melt away sunscreen, makeup, and all the grime from the day. I massage it onto dry skin for a full minute, really working it into my pores. When I add water, it emulsifies into a milky texture and rinses clean.

Step 2: Water-Based Cleanser (Double Cleanse Part 2)

Then I follow with my gentle cleanser again to make sure everything is truly clean. Double cleansing changed my skin more than any single product ever has. If you take one thing from this post, let it be this.

Step 3: Exfoliant (2-3 Times a Week)

I alternate between a glycolic acid toner and a salicylic acid treatment. The glycolic helps with texture and brightness, while the salicylic keeps my pores clear. I never use both on the same night — that's a recipe for irritation.

Step 4: Hyaluronic Acid Serum

I apply this to damp skin (very important!) to lock in hydration. Hyaluronic acid draws moisture into the skin, and it's made the biggest difference in how plump and bouncy my skin looks. I layer 2-3 thin layers for extra hydration.

Step 5: Rich Night Cream

At night, I go heavy. A thick, nourishing night cream that works while I sleep. I look for ingredients like ceramides, peptides, and shea butter. I wake up with the softest skin every single morning.

Weekly Treatments

Once a week, I do a clay mask to deep clean my pores, followed by a hydrating sheet mask to replenish moisture. I call it my "spa Sunday" and it's genuinely the highlight of my week. I light a candle, put on some worship music, and just take that time for myself.

My Biggest Skincare Tips

  • Consistency over products — A simple routine done every day will always beat an elaborate routine done occasionally.
  • Introduce one product at a time — Wait at least 2 weeks before adding something new so you know what's working (and what's causing breakouts).
  • Drink your water — Hydration starts from within. I aim for at least 2 liters a day.
  • Change your pillowcase weekly — This one small change reduced my cheek breakouts dramatically.
  • Be patient — Real results take 6-8 weeks minimum. Don't give up after a week!

Final Thoughts

Your skincare routine doesn't have to be expensive or complicated. It just has to be consistent and right for your skin type. Start with the basics — cleanser, moisturizer, sunscreen — and build from there. Pay attention to how your skin responds and adjust accordingly.

And remember, the best skincare routine is the one you'll actually do every day. Keep it enjoyable, keep it simple, and let your skin do its thing.
